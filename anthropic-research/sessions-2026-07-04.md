### 摘要
OpenAI 向美国政府提议将其 5% 股权交由类似阿拉斯加永久基金的主权财富载体持有，并建议 Anthropic、Google、Meta 等主要 AI 公司跟进，换取政府在政策层面提供支持。该提议市值约达 426 亿美元，被广泛解读为通过让政府成为利益相关方来分散监管压力、换取技术商业化许可的战略布局。协议落地或需国会立法授权，其他公司是否跟进尚不明朗。

### Summary
The Financial Times reported on July 2, 2026 that OpenAI is formally proposing to give the US government a 5% equity stake in the company — worth roughly $42.6 billion at OpenAI's recent $852 billion valuation — through a sovereign wealth vehicle modelled on the Alaska Permanent Fund. CEO Sam Altman framed the move as the most equitable way to share AI's economic upside with the public. Altman is simultaneously pitching that other leading US AI companies — Anthropic, Google, and Meta — cede similar stakes to the same fund. The proposal was timed to defuse political blowback following the GPT-5.6 release delay at the US government's request, and comes as the White House is finalising a voluntary AI release standards framework (August 1 deadline). Any formal implementation would likely require an act of Congress, and it is not yet clear whether Anthropic or Google would agree. The plan has precedent in OpenAI's existing US government relationships but represents a qualitatively new step toward formal public ownership of the leading AI labs.

### Main Takeaway
OpenAI is proposing to make the US government a shareholder in the AI race — a move that would fundamentally entangle regulatory oversight with financial interest, and that Anthropic may be pressured to match.
