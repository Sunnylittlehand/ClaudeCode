## [How Claude Code Is Used in Practice: Agentic Coding and Persistent Returns to Expertise](https://www.anthropic.com/research/claude-code-expertise)
**Source**: Anthropic Research
**Type**: Research Paper
**Date**: 2026-06-16
**Links**: [Anthropic Research](https://www.anthropic.com/research/claude-code-expertise) · [TechTimes coverage](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318955/20260623/ai-coding-agents-reward-domain-expertise-not-coding-skill-anthropic-study-400k-sessions.htm)

### Speaker / Author
Zoe Hitzig, Maxim Massenkoff, Eva Lyubich, Ryan Heller, and Peter McCrory — Anthropic research economists and social scientists. This is an independent empirical study (not a marketing piece), drawing on privacy-preserving analysis of real Claude Code usage data from 235,000 users across all occupations and industries.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐⭐ — The most data-grounded picture yet of who actually benefits from AI coding agents and how, with findings that directly challenge assumptions underlying most AI coding rollout programmes.

### 摘要
Anthropic研究人员分析了约40万次Claude Code会话数据，发现领域专业知识（而非编程背景）才是决定AI辅助编程成效的关键变量。专家用户每条指令能驱动Claude执行的操作数是新手的2.4倍；软件工程师以外的各类职业在编程任务的成功率上与工程师基本持平。这一发现对Sunny的企业推广具有重要意义——投行中的非技术专业人员同样可以高效使用Claude Code。

### Summary
Anthropic published a peer-quality research paper analysing approximately 400,000 Claude Code sessions from 235,000 users (October 2025 to April 2026), with a striking headline finding: coding background is not the primary determinant of success with AI coding agents — domain expertise is. Expert users trigger 12 Claude actions and 3,200 words of output per prompt versus 5 actions and 600 words for novices — a 2.4× gap entirely explained by expertise depth, not role. Every major non-developer occupation (finance, marketing, research, legal) succeeds at coding tasks at nearly the same rate as software engineers. The share of sessions spent debugging broken code fell from 33% to 19% over the six months studied, while operating software and data analysis roughly doubled, signalling a maturation in how people use AI coding agents.

### Main Takeaway
Non-coders succeed at AI-assisted coding at the same rate as software engineers — what predicts success is domain depth, not technical background, fundamentally changing the case for broad enterprise rollout.

### Relevance to Sunny
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ — This is the most important piece of evidence Sunny could present to her investment bank's change management programme. The finding that analysts, researchers, and finance professionals succeed at AI coding tasks at the same rate as engineers directly challenges the "Claude Code is for developers only" objection she likely faces from HR, L&D, and middle management. She should use this research to justify broader rollout beyond just the engineering function — particularly to quantitative analysts, risk modellers, and compliance tech teams who have deep domain expertise in their own disciplines.
