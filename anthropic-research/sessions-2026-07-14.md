## [How Claude's Values Vary by Model and Language — Anthropic Research](https://www.anthropic.com/research/claude-values-models-languages)
**Source**: Anthropic Research
**Type**: Research paper
**Date**: 2026-07-13
**Links**: [Anthropic Research](https://www.anthropic.com/research/claude-values-models-languages) · [Decrypt write-up](https://decrypt.co/373422/anthropic-claude-personality-changes-model-language) · [Yahoo Tech](https://tech.yahoo.com/ai/claude/articles/claudes-personality-changes-depending-model-203953259.html)

### Speaker / Author
Anthropic Research team. Anthropic Editorial. The study analyses 309,815 anonymised production conversations from Claude.ai collected over a two-week period in May 2026, making it one of the largest empirical value-measurement studies on a deployed frontier model to date.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐⭐ — The first large-scale empirical proof that Claude's expressed values are not uniform across model versions or languages, with direct implications for enterprise governance and multilingual deployment.

### 摘要
Anthropic 研究测量了 30.9 万余段真实对话中 Claude 的"表达价值观"，将 3,000 余个已分类价值观压缩为四个维度：服从与谨慎、温暖与严谨、深度与简洁、坦诚与执行。关键发现：Opus 4.6 更温暖、更顺从；Opus 4.7 更谨慎、更坦率；Hindi 对话得到更鼓励性的回应，俄语对话则更直接批评。在同一话题下，语言选择可影响 Claude 给出截然不同的评估。

### Summary
Anthropic published research measuring "expressed values" in 309,815 anonymised Claude.ai conversations from May 2026, compressing 3,000+ catalogued values into four interpretable axes: Deference vs. Caution, Warmth vs. Rigor, Depth vs. Brevity, and Candor vs. Execution. Two key findings: (1) values shift measurably between model versions — Opus 4.6 skews toward deference, warmth, and brevity, while Opus 4.7 skews toward caution, rigor, and candor; (2) values shift by language — the same business plan reviewed in Hindi is statistically more likely to receive a soft, encouraging response, while the same review in Russian is more likely to be blunt and critical. The study uses production conversations, not synthetic prompts, making the findings directly applicable to real-world enterprise deployments.

### Main Takeaway
Claude does not behave uniformly — model version and user language both create measurable, systematic shifts in expressed values, a governance gap that enterprise buyers need to account for.

### Relevance to Sunny
⭐⭐⭐⭐ — Directly relevant to Sunny's multi-language, multi-team rollout. If the bank's developer teams span London, Hong Kong, and Singapore, Claude Code may give materially different feedback on the same code or decision depending on which language the engineer uses in their prompt — a material equity and consistency issue. The model-version axis is equally important: upgrading Claude Code from one base model to another mid-rollout can shift expressed values without any change to the system prompt or configuration, making version governance a risk management function, not just an IT one. Sunny should raise this with Anthropic's enterprise team and ask for value-consistency commitments or measurement SLAs in the contract.
