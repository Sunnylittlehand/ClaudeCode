**Source**: Fortune / TechBuzz / Crypto Briefing
**Type**: Policy / Regulatory Commentary
**Date**: 2026-07-15 to 2026-07-16
**Links**: [TechBuzz](https://www.techbuzz.ai/articles/anthropic-breaks-with-openai-on-illinois-ai-liability-law) · [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/04/17/illinois-openai-anthropic-ai-catastrophe-liability-bills/) · [Crypto Briefing](https://cryptobriefing.com/anthropic-pushes-state-level-ai-safety-laws-diverging-from-openais-national/)

### Speaker / Author
Anthropic Policy Team and OpenAI Policy Team, diverging publicly for the first time on a major AI liability question. The disagreement centres on the Illinois legislature and is being closely watched as a bellwether for how AI labs will position themselves on liability as they approach IPO.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐⭐ — The first publicly visible and direct policy split between Anthropic and OpenAI, with material implications for enterprise procurement and regulated-industry risk framing.

### 摘要
Anthropic 与 OpenAI 在伊利诺伊州 AI 责任立法上首次公开分裂：OpenAI 支持 SB 3444（大规模伤害豁免条款），Anthropic 则支持 SB 3261（要求公布安全计划 + 建立事故报告机制）。这是两大头部 AI 公司在政策立场上的首次明显背离，也标志着 Anthropic 在监管姿态上更靠近欧盟框架，对金融业采购商选择供应商具有重要参考价值。

### Summary
OpenAI is backing Illinois SB 3444, which would exempt frontier AI developers from liability even if their systems cause death or serious injury to 100+ people or more than $1 billion in property damage. Anthropic is instead supporting SB 3261, which requires AI developers to publish a public safety and child protection plan and creates an incident reporting system for catastrophic risks. The split is notable because both companies had previously moved in lockstep on policy — both pushing for federal AI regulation and positioning themselves as responsible actors. Anthropic's position aligns more closely with the EU AI Act's incident-reporting and transparency philosophy, while OpenAI's lobbying posture prioritises a national liability shield over state-level accountability mechanisms.

### Main Takeaway
Anthropic is staking out a more regulatorily accountable position than OpenAI — choosing transparency and incident disclosure over a liability shield — which has direct implications for how regulated enterprises should differentiate between the two vendors.

⭐⭐⭐⭐ — This split matters for Sunny's vendor governance narrative inside the bank. ### Speaker / Author
Anthropic Engineering. This is the Week 32 Claude Code release, continuing the weekly cadence of stability and governance improvements.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐ — Meaningful operational improvements for teams running multi-agent workflows, particularly the fix for subagents silently failing on rate-limit errors and the new streaming transparency flag.

### 摘要
Claude Code v2.1.211 新增 `--forward-subagent-text` 标志以向流式 JSON 输出暴露子 Agent 的文本与思维链，修复了 API 中途过载时流式响应被丢弃的问题，以及子 Agent 遭遇速率限制时静默失败的 Bug，涵盖 Chrome、Windows、Bedrock、Vertex 等平台的广泛稳定性修复。

### Summary
Claude Code v2.1.211 adds a `--forward-subagent-text` flag (and `CLAUDE_CODE_FORWARD_SUBAGENT_TEXT` env var) to include subagent text and thinking in stream-json output — important for teams building observability dashboards over agentic workflows. The release fixes streaming responses being silently discarded when the API emits a mid-stream overloaded/server error (partial output is now preserved with an incomplete-response notice), and fixes subagents that were silently failing rather than returning partial work when cut off by a rate limit or server error. Broad reliability fixes span Chrome, Windows, Bedrock, Vertex, hooks, and session recovery.

### Main Takeaway
The silent-failure fixes for subagents under rate limits are the most operationally critical change — agentic workflows in banks will frequently hit rate limits, and silent drops were a serious reliability gap.

### Relevance to Sunny
⭐⭐⭐ — The subagent silent-failure fixes are directly relevant to any multi-agent Claude Code workflow Sunny's developer teams are running. Before this fix, a subagent hitting a rate limit would silently drop work rather than surfacing the partial result to its parent — a hard-to-diagnose failure mode in regulated workflows where auditability matters. The new `--forward-subagent-text` flag also enables teams to pipe subagent reasoning into centrally-collected logs, which supports the kind of audit-trail capture that FCA/PRA model governance frameworks expect.
