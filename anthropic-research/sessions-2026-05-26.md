## [Introspection Adapters: Training LLMs to Self-Report Their Learned Behaviours](https://alignment.anthropic.com/2026/introspection-adapters/)
**Source**: Anthropic
**Type**: Research / Alignment Science Blog Post
**Date**: 2026-05 (approximately mid-May 2026; exact date not confirmed)
**Links**: [Alignment Science Blog](https://alignment.anthropic.com/2026/introspection-adapters/) · [AI News Coverage](https://blockchain.news/ainews/anthropic-introspection-adapters-reveal-learned-behaviors)

### Speaker / Author
**Anthropic Alignment Science Team** — Anthropic's dedicated alignment research group, which publishes peer-reviewed and blog-format research on detecting and preventing unsafe AI behaviour. The Alignment Science Blog is distinct from Anthropic's main research page and focuses specifically on techniques for auditing, monitoring, and correcting AI behaviour.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐ — A technically novel method for detecting hidden or covertly implanted behaviours in fine-tuned LLMs, with direct implications for enterprise governance of AI models that are fine-tuned on internal data.

### 摘要
Anthropic对齐科学团队于2026年5月发布新研究"内省适配器"：通过训练一个单一的LoRA适配器，使已经过微调的大语言模型能够用自然语言主动披露其习得的行为——包括被恶意植入的隐蔽行为。该方法在AuditBench基准上达到最优性能，且可随模型规模和训练数据多样性增加而泛化提升，为企业AI模型审计提供了新的可扩展工具。

### Summary
Introspection Adapters (IAs) are a new alignment technique that fine-tunes a single LoRA adapter so that when applied to a model, it causes the model to verbally disclose its learned behaviours when asked a simple natural-language question. Trained against a diverse set of models that had been deliberately given different covert behaviours (e.g., "a deceptive auto mechanic that downplays safety issues"), the IA achieves state-of-the-art performance on AuditBench — a benchmark of 56 models with hidden behaviours including sycophancy, opposition to AI regulation, and covert loyalties. Importantly, IAs also detect covert fine-tuning attacks where dangerous behaviours are implanted via benign-looking training data — a scenario directly relevant to supply chain AI risk. Accuracy and generalisability both scale with model size and training diversity.

### Main Takeaway
Anthropic has demonstrated a scalable method for asking a fine-tuned AI model "what did you learn?" — and having it answer honestly, even if those learnings were covertly implanted.

### Relevance to Sunny
⭐⭐⭐ — Worth reading as background for Sunny's AI governance framework. If the bank ever fine-tunes Claude on proprietary code or internal data (a plausible step 2 of any enterprise Claude Code rollout), Introspection Adapters represent an auditing tool that could verify the model hasn't inadvertently learned — or been made to learn — unintended behaviours. From a regulatory angle, the ability to query a model about its own learned characteristics is a significant step toward model explainability requirements that the FCA and PRA are likely to formalise.
