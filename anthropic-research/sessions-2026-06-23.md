**Date**: 2026-06-08 (privacy policy updated) · 2026-07-08 (effective date)

### Speaker / Author
Anthropic Editorial. The policy update was published by Anthropic's legal and trust-and-safety team as a revision to the consumer privacy policy, effective July 8, 2026. The KYC processing is handled by Persona (a third-party identity verification vendor) on Anthropic's behalf.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐⭐ — The most substantive revision to Anthropic's consumer privacy policy since September 2025; introduces biometric data collection for the first time and signals that Anthropic is treating its most capable consumer-facing AI as a regulated-access product rather than an open utility.

### 摘要
Anthropic 将于7月8日起对 Claude 消费者账户（Free、Pro、Max 套餐）实施 KYC 身份核验，可要求用户提交政府签发证件照、自拍照及面部几何数据。核验由第三方服务商 Persona 处理。重要例外：Claude Team、Enterprise 及 API 商业合同账户不受此政策约束。Anthropic 称推动此举的是 Claude 日益强大的智能体能力——长流程任务、第三方应用接入等——产生了对真实身份确认的实际需求。

### Summary
Anthropic updated its consumer privacy policy on June 8, taking effect July 8, to allow mandatory identity checks on Claude Free, Pro, and Max accounts. Where triggered, users must provide a government-issued photo ID, a live selfie, and submit to facial geometry processing — data held by Persona (a third-party KYC vendor), not by Anthropic's own systems. Enterprise, Team, and API commercial accounts are explicitly excluded from this policy. Anthropic states the driver is the expanded capability surface of Claude: longer agentic tasks, third-party integrations, and broader data flows that create genuine accountability needs.

### Main Takeaway
Anthropic is treating frontier consumer AI access as a verified-identity product from July 8 — a quiet but significant shift toward regulated-platform norms.

### Relevance to Sunny
⭐⭐⭐ — Worth reading for background. The Enterprise exclusion is the critical line: Sunny's developer teams on seat-based Enterprise plans are not affected. However, any bank employee using a personal Claude Pro or Max account for work purposes — a shadow-IT risk Sunny may already be managing — will be subject to biometric verification. This could either push shadow users onto official Enterprise seats (a positive) or onto competitor tools (a risk). The introduction of KYC at the consumer tier also sets a governance precedent that regulators like the FCA may eventually ask banks to match for internal AI access, making this worth including in Sunny's horizon-scanning materials.

## [Dario Amodei "Policy on the AI Exponential" — Advanced AI Framework: FAA-Style Regulation and Government Blocking Power](https://www.anthropic.com/policy-on-the-ai-exponential)
**Source**: Anthropic
**Type**: Blog Post / Policy Paper
**Date**: 2026-06-10
**Links**: [Anthropic policy page](https://www.anthropic.com/policy-on-the-ai-exponential) · [Dario Amodei's personal post](https://darioamodei.com/post/policy-on-the-ai-exponential) · [VentureBeat: FAA-style regulation](https://venturebeat.com/technology/anthropic-ceo-calls-for-faa-style-regulation-of-powerful-ai-models-what-enterprises-should-know) · [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/anthropic-advanced-ai-framework-government-block-deployments)

### Speaker / Author
Dario Amodei, Anthropic CEO and co-founder. Published June 10 alongside the Economic Policy Framework (already captured in the ledger on 2026-06-20). This entry covers the Advanced AI Framework — the safety-governance half — which proposes a binding regulatory regime for frontier AI comparable in structure to FAA airworthiness certification.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐⭐ — The most detailed regulatory proposal yet from any frontier lab CEO, and one that explicitly calls for mandatory third-party safety testing and government authority to block dangerous deployments — proposals that, if enacted, would reshape the compliance landscape for every regulated-industry AI deployment.

### 摘要
达里奥·阿莫德在《AI 指数级增长政策》中提出"先进 AI 框架"：建议对计算量超过 10²⁵ FLOP、或 AI 收入超 5 亿美元/R&D 超 10 亿美元的模型实施强制第三方安全测评，覆盖生物武器、网络攻击、失控风险及 AI 自动科研四大风险域；政府应获得法律授权，对具有灾难性危害风险的模型叫停部署，违者面临与全球年收入挂钩的阶梯式民事罚款。

### Summary
Dario Amodei's "Policy on the AI Exponential" paper (June 10) is a two-part policy proposal; the previously captured Economic Policy Framework addressed labour market impacts, while this Advanced AI Framework proposes a binding safety regime for the most powerful AI models. The framework would apply to models trained above 10²⁵ floating-point operations, or companies with more than $500 million in AI revenue or more than $1 billion in R&D. It mandates independent third-party safety testing across four priority risk categories — biological weapons, offensive cyber operations, loss of control of AI systems, and automated R&D that could accelerate the other three. Crucially, it proposes that governments receive statutory authority to block or deter deployments that fail these evaluations, with civil penalties tied to global annual revenue that escalate with repeat violations. Amodei draws an explicit analogy to FAA airworthiness certification: you do not ground all aviation, but no aircraft flies without passing a safety gate.

### Main Takeaway
Anthropic's CEO is asking governments to be able to ground dangerous AI models the same way the FAA grounds unsafe aircraft — and willing to accept that authority applying to Anthropic itself.

### Relevance to Sunny
⭐⭐⭐⭐ — High value for Sunny's regulatory horizon-scanning. If this framework — or a version of it — is adopted in the UK or EU, banks deploying frontier AI tools will be operating within a formal safety-certification regime analogous to existing model risk management frameworks (SS1/23, SR 11-7).
