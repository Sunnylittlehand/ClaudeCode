### Speaker / Author
Anthropic Research Team. This paper is based on a privacy-preserving analysis of approximately 400,000 Claude Code sessions from October 2025 to April 2026 — the largest empirical study of real-world agentic coding behaviour published to date by any lab.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐⭐ — First large-scale empirical study of who benefits most from agentic coding and how human-AI collaboration actually works in practice; directly informs how organisations should staff and train for Claude Code.

### 摘要
Anthropic于2026年6月16日发布基于约40万个Claude Code会话（2025年10月至2026年4月）的大规模实证研究，揭示了专业知识在智能体编程中的持续回报规律。研究发现：用户带入会话的领域专业知识越深，Claude每条指令所完成的工作量越大，会话成功率也越高。典型会话中，用户负责大多数规划决策（做什么），Claude负责大多数执行决策（如何做）。此外，开发者虽在约60%的工作中使用AI，但报告称能"完全委托"的任务仅占0%至20%，这一显著落差揭示了自主化程度仍有巨大提升空间。

### Summary
Published on 16 June 2026, this research paper presents findings from ~400,000 Claude Code sessions over six months, introducing the concept of "persistent returns to expertise": the greater a user's domain expertise going into a session, the more work Claude completes per instruction and the more often the session ends in success. In a typical session, humans make most planning decisions (what to do) while Claude makes most execution decisions (how to do it). The study also finds that coding success rates are nearly identical across all major occupations — software engineers, scientists, social scientists — suggesting Claude Code is genuinely accessible across the workforce. A striking gap was identified between actual AI use (~60% of developer work involves AI) and full delegation (only 0–20% of tasks are fully handed off), pointing to a large headroom for autonomous capability improvement.

### Main Takeaway
Domain expertise still pays off at scale with Claude Code — the tool amplifies what you already know rather than making everyone equal.
