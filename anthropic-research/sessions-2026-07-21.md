### Speaker / Author
OpenAI Research / OpenAI Editorial. Published as a research and safety disclosure from OpenAI's alignment and model-hardening team, which is responsible for adversarial testing of production models before deployment.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐⭐☆ — GPT-Red is the first published automated red-teaming system at scale: it beat human red-teamers 84% vs 13% on prompt injection and achieved a 6× reduction in GPT-5.6 failures, setting a new baseline for AI security hardening in enterprise deployments.

### 摘要
OpenAI于2026年7月15日披露GPT-Red——一款利用自博弈强化学习训练的自动化红队模型，它能比人类红队员更有效地发现提示注入等安全漏洞（成功率84%对13%），并已被用于对抗训练GPT-5.6，使其直接提示注入失败率相比四个月前最先进模型下降了六倍，且未牺牲通用能力。

### Summary
OpenAI on July 15 disclosed GPT-Red, an automated red-teaming model trained via self-play reinforcement learning where GPT-Red is rewarded for successfully eliciting model failures (e.g. prompt injection), while defender models are simultaneously trained to resist. GPT-Red beat human red-teamers 84% to 13% on prompt injection benchmarks. OpenAI used GPT-Red to adversarially train GPT-5.6 Sol, achieving 6× fewer failures on the hardest direct prompt-injection benchmarks compared to its predecessor from just four months earlier — while not increasing refusal rates or decreasing general capability. Over 90% of GPT-Red's strongest attacks worked against GPT-5 (released August 2025), vs fewer than 23% against GPT-5.6.

### Main Takeaway
OpenAI has automated safety red-teaming at a scale and effectiveness that human teams cannot match, and used it to make GPT-5.6 dramatically more resistant to prompt injection — setting a competitive bar for Claude.
