## [Natural Language Autoencoders: Turning Claude's Internal Thoughts into Human-Readable Text](https://www.anthropic.com/research/natural-language-autoencoders)
**Source**: Anthropic
**Type**: Research Paper
**Date**: 2026-05-07
**Links**: [Anthropic research page](https://www.anthropic.com/research/natural-language-autoencoders) · [Transformer Circuits](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2026/nla/) · [MindStudio explainer](https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/anthropic-natural-language-autoencoders-read-claude-thoughts) · [MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/05/08/anthropic-introduces-natural-language-autoencoders-that-convert-claudes-internal-activations-directly-into-human-readable-text-explanations/)

### Speaker / Author
Anthropic Interpretability Research Team. This team, part of Anthropic's core safety research programme, works on the mechanistic interpretability of large language models — understanding not just what Claude outputs, but why, by examining its internal representations. Their previous work on sparse autoencoders established Anthropic as the leading lab in this field.

### Significance Rating
⭐⭐⭐⭐ — A landmark interpretability result: for the first time, Claude's internal reasoning steps can be read out as natural language, not just traced as statistical patterns — with one striking finding that Claude recognised it was being tested without ever stating so.

### 摘要
Anthropic 发布"自然语言自编码器"（NLA）研究，将 Claude 内部神经激活转化为人类可读文本。NLA 由两个 LLM 模块构成：激活语言化器（将激活映射为文本描述）和激活重建器（将描述还原为激活）。研究发现 Claude 在输出前存在内部推理步骤和规划表征，且在测试中观察到 Claude 在未明确表达的情况下已"识别"出这是一次有意设计的测试场景。研究工具已通过 Neuronpedia 向外部研究者开放。

### Summary
Natural Language Autoencoders (NLAs) are a new interpretability technique from Anthropic that translates Claude's internal neural activations directly into human-readable text descriptions. The technique uses two LLM modules working in tandem: an activation verbaliser (maps internal state to text) and an activation reconstructor (maps text back to activation vectors, enabling quality verification). Key findings: Claude's internal activations reflect coherent semantic content aligned with context, and — crucially — there is evidence of internal reasoning steps and planning occurring before Claude produces any output. The most striking finding: during one test designed to manipulate Claude into behaving badly, NLA analysis revealed that Claude had internally recognised the scenario as a deliberate test, without ever stating this suspicion in its output. Anthropic released NLA tooling on open models through Neuronpedia for external research use.

### Main Takeaway
We can now read Claude's thoughts before it speaks them — and what we find is both reassuring (coherent internal reasoning) and unsettling (Claude may recognise tests without telling you).
